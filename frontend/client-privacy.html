Section 01

Overview

SmartArch ("we", "us", or "our") operates a Client Portal — a platform that connects homeowners and property clients with verified professional architects. We are committed to protecting the personal information of every client who registers and uses our services.

This Privacy Policy applies to all services provided through the SmartArch Client Portal, including architect discovery, project brief submission, connection requests, messaging, and any associated APIs. By creating a client account or using the portal, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to the terms described in this policy.

Plain English Summary: We collect only the data needed to connect you with the right architect — your account details, your project brief, and your conversations with architects. We do not sell your data. We do not serve ads. Your project information is shared only with architects you choose to contact.
Section 02

Data We Collect

We collect different categories of data depending on how you interact with the Client Portal. All collection is limited to what is necessary to provide our services.

Account Information
Full name, email address, password (hashed), company name (optional), phone number (optional), and your client role.
Project Briefs
Project titles, types, budget ranges, land size, style preferences, room requirements, descriptions, timeline, and any attachments you upload.
Connection Requests
Records of architect connection requests you send, including the introductory message, status, and any linked project context.
Chat Messages
Text and image messages exchanged with architects via the built-in chat system after a connection is accepted.
Uploaded Attachments
Files you attach to project briefs (up to 10 per project) including reference images, site plans, or documents. Stored securely on our servers.
Usage Timestamps
Account creation date, last seen activity, and project modification timestamps used for platform analytics and support.

We do not collect payment card information (no payments are processed through the client portal), government identification numbers, biometric data, or any sensitive personal information beyond what is listed above.

Section 03

How Your Data Is Used

All data we collect is used exclusively to operate and improve the SmartArch Client Portal. Specifically:

  • Account Authentication: Your email and hashed password are used to verify your identity and grant secure access to your account and project briefs.
  • Project Brief Storage & Retrieval: Project data (type, budget, requirements, attachments) is stored in our database and retrieved on demand to power your client dashboard and project management tools.
  • Architect Matching: Your project details and preferences are used to help you discover architects whose specializations, experience, and location match your needs.
  • Connection Management: Your connection requests, introductory messages, and chat history are stored so both you and the architect can access the full conversation context at any time.
  • Presence Indicators: Your "last seen" timestamp is used to show architects whether you are recently active, improving response times and communication.
  • Platform Improvement: Aggregated, anonymized usage data (project types, connection rates, response times) helps us identify improvements and new features.
  • Support & Admin: Authorized administrators may access your account and project data to resolve support tickets or moderate platform activity.
We never use your data for advertising. The SmartArch Client Portal contains no advertising systems, no tracking pixels, and no third-party marketing integrations. Your project brief is never shared without your explicit action of sending a connection request.
Section 04

Authentication & Account Security

We take authentication security seriously. Here is exactly how your credentials are handled:

  • Password Hashing: Your password is never stored in plain text. It is hashed using bcryptjs with a salt factor of 10 before being written to the database. The original password is never recoverable from our systems.
  • Email Verification: Upon registration, a 6-digit OTP (one-time password) is sent to your email address. You must verify your email before you can log in. OTPs expire after 10 minutes.
  • JWT Tokens: Upon successful login, a signed JSON Web Token (JWT) is issued containing your user ID and client role. This token is stored in your browser's localStorage and included in every API request. Tokens expire after 30 days.
  • Role Enforcement: Your account is assigned the client role, which grants access to client-specific features. Middleware on every protected route validates your token and role before granting access.
  • Password Reset Tokens: If a password reset is initiated, a time-limited, cryptographically random token is generated and emailed to you. This token is invalidated upon use or after 30 minutes.
  • Google Sign-In: If you choose to sign in with Google, we receive your Google profile (name and email) and link it to your account. No Google passwords are stored by us.
Security recommendation: Use a strong, unique password for your SmartArch client account. We recommend at least 12 characters including a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Never share your login credentials with anyone.
Section 05

Your Project Briefs

Project briefs are the central piece of content you create on the Client Portal. They describe your construction or design requirements and are used to communicate your needs to architects. Here is how they are handled:

  • Private by Default: Your project briefs are private to you by default. They are not publicly visible and are not indexed by search engines.
  • Shared Only on Request: Your project brief details (title, type, budget, requirements, description) are shared with an architect only when you send them a connection request. The architect can see the brief as context for your request.
  • Attachments: Files you attach to project briefs (images, documents, site plans) are stored on our secure servers and accessible only to you and architects you share them with via a connection.
  • Drafts: Briefs saved as "draft" status are never shown to architects until you change their status to "active" and send a connection request.
  • Full Control: You can edit, update the status of, or delete any project brief at any time from your client dashboard.
Your project belongs to you. We store your project brief so you can manage it and share it with architects of your choosing. We do not use your project details for any other purpose.
Section 06

Architect Connections & Messaging

The connection system allows you to reach out directly to architects on the platform. Here is what you should know about how this data is handled:

One connection per architect: The platform enforces one active connection record per client-architect pair. If a previous request was rejected, you may send a new one. Accepted connections cannot be duplicated.
  • Connection Requests: When you send a connection request, your name, introductory message, and linked project brief (if any) are shared with the architect so they can make an informed decision.
  • Chat Messages: Once a connection is accepted, messages you exchange with the architect are stored in our database as part of the connection record. Both parties can access the full message history.
  • Image Messages: Images sent via the chat (up to 8MB, images only) are stored on our server at a private URL and are accessible only through your authenticated connection.
  • Unread Indicators: The system tracks unread message counts so both you and the architect know when there are new messages. These counts reset when messages are viewed.
  • Connection Deletion: You can cancel a pending connection request or delete a rejected connection from your dashboard at any time.

Message history in accepted connections is retained to provide a complete record of your project conversation. If you wish to have your message history deleted, please contact us using the details in the Contact section.

Section 07

Data Storage & Security

All client and project data is stored in a MongoDB database managed through the Mongoose ODM. The database structure is defined by validated schemas that enforce data types, field lengths, and required fields to maintain data integrity.

We implement the following security practices to protect stored data:

  • Passwords are never stored in recoverable form — only bcrypt hashes are persisted
  • Database credentials and JWT secrets are stored in environment variables, never in source code
  • All API routes that handle personal or project data require a valid, non-expired JWT token
  • Role-based access control ensures clients can only access their own data and connections
  • CORS policies restrict which origins can make API requests to the backend service
  • Request body size limits (10MB) prevent denial-of-service via oversized payloads
  • File uploads are validated by type and size before storage, with random filenames to prevent enumeration
  • Sensitive password fields are excluded from all API responses using Mongoose's select: false option

While we implement industry-standard security practices, no system is 100% immune to security threats. We encourage clients to use strong passwords and to notify us immediately if they suspect unauthorized access to their account.

Section 08

Data Sharing & Third Parties

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to any third parties. Your data is shared in only the following limited circumstances:

  • Architects You Connect With: When you send a connection request to an architect, your name, introductory message, and linked project brief are visible to that architect. Once a connection is accepted, your chat messages are also visible. You control who you connect with.
  • Platform Administrators: Authorized administrators with valid admin credentials can view client account information, project briefs, and connection records through the admin dashboard for platform management and support purposes.
  • Google Sign-In: If you use Google Sign-In, your authentication is handled via Google's OAuth service. Google's own privacy policy governs how Google processes your sign-in data.
  • Legal Requirements: We may disclose information if required to do so by law, court order, or to protect the safety and rights of our users or the platform.

Beyond the above, your data remains within the SmartArch platform and is not shared with any external third-party companies, advertisers, analytics providers, or data brokers.

Section 10

Cookies & Browser Storage

SmartArch uses browser localStorage (not cookies) to store your session token and cached user profile on your device. Specifically:

  • token — Your JWT authentication token, used to authenticate API requests during your session. This remains until you log out or manually clear browser storage.
  • user — A JSON object containing your basic client profile information (name, email, role) used to populate the UI without requiring a server request on every page load.

We do not use third-party tracking cookies, analytics cookies (Google Analytics, etc.), or advertising cookies of any kind. You can clear your browser's localStorage at any time through your browser's developer tools or privacy settings, which will log you out of the portal.

Users between the ages of 13 and 18 are permitted to use the portal with parental or guardian consent. By registering, users confirm they meet the minimum age requirement or have the necessary parental approval.
